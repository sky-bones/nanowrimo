Top FAQs

Where do I write my novel? Do I type it on the NaNoWriMo website?

You write your novel using your own materials: on your preferred word-processing program, by hand, by typewriter, etc. You do not type it directly on the NaNoWriMo or Camp NaNoWriMo site.

How do I update my word count? How often should I update it?

You can update your word count by entering your total cumulative count in the field on the top of your screen and hitting “Update.” Update your word count as frequently or infrequently as you’d like. We recommend a daily update!

How do I win NaNoWriMo? What are the prizes? Is there an entry fee?

You win NaNoWriMo by writing 50,000 words of your novel between November 1 and November 30. (Or during Camp NaNoWriMo, just reaching your personal word-count goal.) There’s no limit on how many people can win! Just be sure that you’ve defined a novel on our site, and that you validate your novel’s word count at the end of the month.

Every year, we have several generous sponsors that offer participant and winner goodies. Check out the NaNoWriMo prizes here, and Camp NaNoWriMo prizes here!

All programs for National Novel Writing Month, Camp NaNoWriMo, and the Young Writers Program are free. However, we run on donations, and we do ask ably-financed participants to contribute something towards hosting and administrative costs. Because we’re a nonprofit, the donation is tax-deductible!

How do I add or remove a writing buddy?

To add a writing buddy on the NaNoWriMo site, go to the profile of the person you’d like to buddy and click on the “Add as Buddy” button to the right of the username.

Finding a buddy’s profile can be a bit tricky! If you meet someone in the forums, you can click on their username to reach their profile page. If you’re using our search function, be sure to use the exact username you’re searching for; if it’s even a little off, our search functionality won't find it.

To remove a buddy, simply go to his or her profile again and hit the “Remove buddy” button.

Can I participate if I’m not American? Can I write a novel in any language?

Definitely! We’re an international nonprofit with participants around the world.

You’re welcome to write in whichever language you’re most comfortable with. Unfortunately, our word-count validator doesn’t read non-Latin characters properly, so we encourage you to generate an estimated word count using a random-text generator.

The Basics

Why 50,000 words? And how do you define “novel”?

Our experiences since 1999 show that 50,000 is a difficult but doable goal, even for people with full-time jobs and children. The length makes it a short novel (about the length of The Great Gatsby). We don’t use the word “novella” because it doesn’t seem to impress people the way “novel” does.

We define a novel as “a lengthy work of fiction.” Beyond that, we let you decide whether what you’re writing falls under the heading of “novel.” In short: If you believe you’re writing a novel, we believe you’re writing a novel, too.

On Camp NaNoWriMo, we’re open to word counts from 10,000 to 999,999. So bring on those short stories and epics!

How and when can I sign up? When do I start writing?

You can sign up at any time, year-round. To sign up for the upcoming National Novel Writing Month, just click on the “Sign Up” button on the homepage.

You can start writing at the stroke of midnight on November 1.

On Camp NaNoWriMo, you can participate in April and July.

Is there a minimum age to participate?

NaNoWriMo and Camp NaNoWriMo are open to all writers over the age of 13. Our Young Writers Program is open to authors 17 and under.

How can I be sure my novel is protected?

To protect your privacy and rights to your work, none of the novels submitted to our site are read by another human. The text is submitted to our server, run through our word-count validator, and then immediately deleted.

This means we do not keep any copies of your novel, so be sure to back up your hard work!

What do I do with my novel after the month is over?

The only requirement is to congratulate yourself on your hard work.

That said, many NaNoWriMo and Camp NaNoWriMo participants revise their manuscripts—to publish traditionally, to self-publish, or just to share their stories with friends and family. We support this process during our "Now What?" Months.

My Account

If I had an account last year, do I need to re-register?

Nope! All you need is the same username or email address and password you used to sign up in years past.

On Camp NaNoWriMo, you can also use your existing NaNoWriMo login.

For security purposes, our Young Writers Program website is not linked to the NaNoWriMo or Camp NaNoWriMo sites. To participate on those sites, you can create a new account with the same email address you use on YWP.

Can I change my username?

No, you cannot currently change your username.

You can delete your current account on the “Account Settings” page, and then create a new one 30 days after deletion. (Your email address will be freed up once the deletion is permanent.)

Can I change my email address?

Yes! You can update your email address by logging in to your account and going to “Account Settings” in the My NaNoWriMo menu. There, you’ll see an editable text box to change your email address.

How do I recover my password or username?

You can log in with the email address you used to sign up. If you lost your password, you can request a new one to be sent to you.

If you no longer have access to the original email address you used to sign up and have forgotten your password, you’ll need to start over with a new account.

How do I unsubscribe from emails?

First, let us apologize! We do our best not to spam your inbox, or send you any emails that you don’t want to receive.

To manage your updates, click “Account Settings” in the My NaNoWriMo menu, and uncheck the appropriate boxes under “Which updates would you like to get via e-mail?”

On Camp NaNoWriMo, uncheck “Receive Camp-related emails” on the “User Settings” page in the Camper Profile menu.

How do I delete my NaNoWriMo account?

To delete your account:

  1. 1. Click on “Account Settings” in the My NaNoWriMo menu.
  2. 2. Click the “Delete My Account” button at the bottom of the page.
  3. 3. State your reason (if you don't mind), and all your account information will be permanently deleted after 30 days.
Having second thoughts? Just log in again within 30 days of your delete request, and everything should be just as you left it. (Be sure to pay close attention to the date! We won't be able to recover permanently deleted account information.)

My Profile

How do I post, edit, or delete a novel excerpt or synopsis?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, you can find your projects under “My Writing Projects” in the My Camp NaNo menu.

To enter or update your novel title, excerpt, or synopsis, go to the “My Novels” page in the My NaNoWriMo menu.

If you’re entering your novel, choose the current event for which you wish to define your novel (for example, "November 2015"). Fill in any of the fields you'd like; all are optional. Then click the “Create Novel” button. This will bring you to the Novel page where you can see your new work-in-progress!

To change or update any of that information, just click the “Edit” button on your “My Novels” page.

How do I post, edit, or delete a book cover?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, you can find your projects under “My Writing Projects” in the My Camp NaNo menu, then follow steps 2-4 from there.

We recommend using the JPG format for most images on this site. When you upload, the image file will be resized. For best results, try to make your image fit the size requirements: 230 x 300 pixels.

If you wish to remove the book cover, click the “Delete cover” checkbox under the image on your “Project Info” page. When you press “Save,” the image will be removed from the server.

To enter or update your novel’s cover on NaNoWriMo:
  1. 1. Click on “My Novels” in the My NaNoWriMo menu.
  2. 2. Click the “Edit” button within the novel you wish to add a cover to.
  3. 3. Click “Choose a file.”
  4. 4. Select an image saved on your computer.

How do I add or remove a writing buddy?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, check out our cabin system for connecting with writing buddies.

To add a writing buddy on the NaNoWriMo site, go to the profile of the person you’d like to buddy and click on the “Add as Buddy” button to the right of the username.

Finding a buddy’s profile can be a bit tricky! If you meet someone in the forums, you can click on their username to reach their profile page. If you’re using our search function, be sure to use the exact username you’re searching for; if it’s even a little off, our search functionality won't find it.

To remove a buddy, simply go to his or her profile again and hit the “Remove buddy” button.

What is a “halo”?

Halos — and the Brought to You By Page — are our way of thanking our donors on our event sites! When you see a shiny yellow Halo on someone's profile picture, that means they've pledged their support to our writing programs.

After a minimum donation of $10 through our Donation Station, you’ll get your very own halo to show off on your profile page and in the forums.

Where is my “halo”?

Halos will show up on your profile for the calendar year in which you donated. For example, if you donate in October, it will remain on your profile until early February. If you donate in January, your halo will appear in early February when we update our fundraising year.

If you've just made a donation and your Halo doesn’t appear after two business days, contact the store directly with your order number and username.

My Novel

Where do I write my novel? Do I type it on the NaNoWriMo website?

You write your novel using your own materials: on your preferred word-processing program, by hand, by typewriter, etc. You do not type it directly on the NaNoWriMo or Camp NaNoWriMo site.

Do I have to start my novel from scratch on the first of the month? Can I use an outline?

Traditionally, NaNoWriMo works best when you start a brand-new project. It may be an arbitrary distinction, but we’ve seen that novelists do better (and have more fun) when they’re free from the constraints of existing manuscripts. Give yourself the gift of a clean slate!

What genres can I write in?

Camp NaNoWriMo is specifically designed for open-ended writing; you can even specify the category of your work! Any genre of “novel” is okay for NaNoWriMo. We just want you to be excited about writing.

What if I don’t finish? Or hit my word-count goal but I’m only halfway through my novel?

Nothing ventured, nothing gained, right? Even if you end the month 49,000 words short, that’s 1,000 words you wouldn’t have otherwise.

Consider the word-count goal a threshold, not necessarily a stopping place. Reaching that goal and realizing you still have a lot of wrapping up to do is a good thing: it gives you something to come back to and work on later. You will still win if you reach your goal but have not yet completed your novel. Keep writing!

How do I update my pre-2007 novels on the site?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, you can display all your past projects by going to the “My Writing Projects” page in the My Camp NaNo menu.

How can I scramble my novel?

If you'd like to scramble your novel's words before submitting it to the website, you can go ahead and use this word scrambler site!

My Word Count

How do I update my word count? How often should I update it?

You can update your word count by entering your total cumulative count in the field on the top of your screen and hitting “Update.” Update your word count as frequently or infrequently as you’d like. We recommend a daily update!

How do I edit a prior day’s word count?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, click on “My Writing Projects” in the My Camp NaNo menu, then “View Statistics” for your current project. Under the word-count graph, click “Edit Word Count by Day” to make changes.

To edit your word count by day on the NaNoWriMo site:

  1. 1. Click on “My Novels” in the My NaNoWriMo menu.
  2. 2. Click the “Stats” button for your current novel.
  3. 3. Click the “Edit Word Count by Day” button. This will bring you to your retroactive word count editing page.
  4. 4. Enter the correct, cumulative total you wrote for that day and press the “Save” button. Your graph should now be correct.

Why is there a discrepancy between my writing program’s word count and NaNoWriMo’s word counter?

No two word-counting devices are exactly the same, and this does lead to some discrepancies.

To check your writing program’s word counter against ours on Camp NaNoWriMo, click “Check your official word count” under the tent on the homepage.

Please be sure to check your count on our validator prior to the end of the month. We don’t want you to be surprised during last-minute validation.

On NaNoWriMo, click “Check Word Count” in the My NaNoWriMo menu.

How and when do I validate my novel to win?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, to validate your novel click “Validate your word count to win” at the top of the homepage. Winning starts on the 20th of the month and goes until the 30th or 31st, at 11:59 PM in your timezone.

Winning begins on the 20th and goes until the end of the month. To validate your novel to win on NaNoWriMo:

  1. 1. Be sure you've created a novel.
  2. 2. Click "I am ready to validate my novel" in at the top of our site, where you normally enter your word count. (You can also find a text link on your novel page.)
  3. 3. Copy the entire text of your 50,000-word novel and paste it into the validator box.
  4. 4. Click "Validate."

How do I validate my novel if I’m writing by hand?

If you’ve written your novel by hand, you can still validate your novel to win. First, have a trusted friend verify the number of words you’ve written. Then, using a random-text generator, submit the same number of words as your handwritten manuscript to our word-count validator.

My Badges

What are badges?

Badges on the NaNoWriMo site are milestone markers, special kudos, and personal descriptors. There are two types of badges: Participation and writing badges, which are automatically rewarded based on your achievements, Personal achievement badges, which are self-assigned.

How do I earn participation and writing badges?

You can earn participation and writing badges by completing specific milestones on the site. For example, when you fill out your profile, update your word count for the first time, or post on the forums, you’ll be awarded the corresponding badge on your NaNoWriMo dashboard.

How do I add personal achievement badges?

Personal achievement badges are badges that can be self-awarded. For example, if you identify as a “Planner” or a “Pantser,” you can assign yourself the corresponding badge.

These differ from participation and writing badges in that they are not system awarded — it’s up to you to “claim” the personal achievement badges you feel you’ve earned (this is on the honor system, of course)!

How do I get winner and participant badges for past novels?

To display your noveling glory of year’s past, you’ll need to enter a novel for that year’s event. To do so,

  1. 1. Log into your account.
  2. 2. Go to “My Novels” under the My NaNoWriMo menu.
  3. 3. Find the year for the novel you’d like to enter.
  4. 4. Click the “Enter It In!” link under the year.
  5. 5. Add in any information you’d like to include. You can be as detailed (or not!) as you like.
Local Events

How can I meet and write with Wrimos in my area?

While there’s a large international community on our website, you can also meet up and write with other writers in your local regions. Most regions have a determined and helpful volunteer (called a Municipal Liaison) who organizes local write-ins.

How do I “home” or affiliate with my local region(s)?

When you first create an account, you are prompted to select a “home” region. (This is completely optional; you do not need to join a region to participate.) You can join as many regions as you like, but only one region can be marked as your home region.

Why isn’t there a region near me?

We are only able to create regions where we have a local volunteer (called a Municipal Liaison) in place to coordinate for the area. If you’re interested in creating a region in your area and becoming and ML, you can find more information on the join us page.

How do I become a Municipal Liaison for my region?

To become a Municipal Liaison for your region, you must be over 18 years of age and have done (and won!) NaNoWriMo at least once.

For more details on duties and expectations for MLs, please check out our Local Volunteers page. We strongly encourage interested applicants to first contact existing MLs if your region already has an ML(s).

If you want to apply to be a Municipal Liaison, applications will be posted on the Local Volunteer page when they are available. If applications have closed for the year, you can sign up there to be notified when the next round of applications are available.

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes! NaNoWriMo, Camp NaNoWriMo, and NaNoWriMo's Young Writers Program are projects of National Novel Writing Month, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Berkeley, California. All donations are fully tax-deductible in the United States.

If you are donating from outside the United States, we recommend you check with the relevant authority in your country about whether or not donations to NaNoWriMo are tax-deductible.

How can I volunteer for NaNoWriMo?

We rely on a full-time staff of paid employees for most of the core work of organizing and facilitating the event. If you’d like to volunteer in your area, please consider being a Municipal Liaison. We also hire seasonal interns and have occasional volunteer opportunities.

Can I partner with NaNoWriMo to promote my product or organization?

Please feel free to get in touch and tell us more about what you do, and how it can help NaNoWriMo participants.

Can I use the NaNoWriMo name and/or logo? What’s under your trademark?

We love that you care about NaNoWriMo enough to want to bring more NaNoWriMo stuff into the world! That said, we’ve put a lot of work into making the NaNoWriMo brand a shining beacon of writing encouragement, and we must be protective of our name and logo.

We have legal protection on the names “National Novel Writing Month” and “NaNoWriMo,” as well as our logo and any derivative products. To help us put as much of our resources as possible into our events, we ask that you adhere to the following usage rules:

Logo: Please do not use our logo or parts of our logo.

Manufactured items: Please do not make any products, free or otherwise, bearing the “NaNoWriMo” or “National Novel Writing Month” name.

Flyers: Please don’t make your own. We have official flyers that we’d love you to use to promote NaNoWriMo.

Web and social media graphics: Please don’t make your own. Every year we craft some NaNo Flair that you can add to your website or social media accounts.

Software and applications: Please do not include “NaNoWriMo” or “National Novel Writing Month” in the name of your software or application.
