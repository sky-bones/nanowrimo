What if I don’t finish? Or hit my word-count goal but I’m only halfway through my novel?

Nothing ventured, nothing gained, right? Even if you end the month 49,000 words short, that’s 1,000 words you wouldn’t have otherwise.

Consider the word-count goal a threshold, not necessarily a stopping place. Reaching that goal and realizing you still have a lot of wrapping up to do is a good thing: it gives you something to come back to and work on later. You will still win if you reach your goal but have not yet completed your novel. Keep writing!

My Word Count

How do I update my word count? How often should I update it?

You can update your word count by entering your total cumulative count in the field on the top of your screen and hitting “Update.” Update your word count as frequently or infrequently as you’d like. We recommend a daily update!

How do I edit a prior day’s word count?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, click on “My Writing Projects” in the My Camp NaNo menu, then “View Statistics” for your current project. Under the word-count graph, click “Edit Word Count by Day” to make changes.

To edit your word count by day on the NaNoWriMo site:

  1. 1. Click on “My Novels” in the My NaNoWriMo menu.
  2. 2. Click the “Stats” button for your current novel.
  3. 3. Click the “Edit Word Count by Day” button. This will bring you to your retroactive word count editing page.
  4. 4. Enter the correct, cumulative total you wrote for that day and press the “Save” button. Your graph should now be correct.

Why is there a discrepancy between my writing program’s word count and NaNoWriMo’s word counter?

No two word-counting devices are exactly the same, and this does lead to some discrepancies.

To check your writing program’s word counter against ours on Camp NaNoWriMo, click “Check your official word count” under the tent on the homepage.

Please be sure to check your count on our validator prior to the end of the month. We don’t want you to be surprised during last-minute validation.

On NaNoWriMo, click “Check Word Count” in the My NaNoWriMo menu.

How and when do I validate my novel to win?

On Camp NaNoWriMo, to validate your novel click “Validate your word count to win” at the top of the homepage. Winning starts on the 20th of the month and goes until the 30th or 31st, at 11:59 PM in your timezone.

Winning begins on the 20th and goes until the end of the month. To validate your novel to win on NaNoWriMo:

  1. 1. Be sure you've created a novel.
  2. 2. Click "I am ready to validate my novel" in at the top of our site, where you normally enter your word count. (You can also find a text link on your novel page.)
  3. 3. Copy the entire text of your 50,000-word novel and paste it into the validator box.
  4. 4. Click "Validate."

How do I validate my novel if I’m writing by hand?

If you’ve written your novel by hand, you can still validate your novel to win. First, have a trusted friend verify the number of words you’ve written. Then, using a random-text generator, submit the same number of words as your handwritten manuscript to our word-count validator.

My Badges

What are badges?

Badges on the NaNoWriMo site are milestone markers, special kudos, and personal descriptors. There are two types of badges: Participation and writing badges, which are automatically rewarded based on your achievements, Personal achievement badges, which are self-assigned.

How do I earn participation and writing badges?

You can earn participation and writing badges by completing specific milestones on the site. For example, when you fill out your profile, update your word count for the first time, or post on the forums, you’ll be awarded the corresponding badge on your NaNoWriMo dashboard.

How do I add personal achievement badges?

Personal achievement badges are badges that can be self-awarded. For example, if you identify as a “Planner” or a “Pantser,” you can assign yourself the corresponding badge.

These differ from participation and writing badges in that they are not system awarded — it’s up to you to “claim” the personal achievement badges you feel you’ve earned (this is on the honor system, of course)!

How do I get winner and participant badges for past novels?

To display your noveling glory of year’s past, you’ll need to enter a novel for that year’s event. To do so,

  1. 1. Log into your account.
  2. 2. Go to “My Novels” under the My NaNoWriMo menu.
  3. 3. Find the year for the novel you’d like to enter.
  4. 4. Click the “Enter It In!” link under the year.
  5. 5. Add in any information you’d like to include. You can be as detailed (or not!) as you like.
Local Events

How can I meet and write with Wrimos in my area?

While there’s a large international community on our website, you can also meet up and write with other writers in your local regions. Most regions have a determined and helpful volunteer (called a Municipal Liaison) who organizes local write-ins.

How do I “home” or affiliate with my local region(s)?

When you first create an account, you are prompted to select a “home” region. (This is completely optional; you do not need to join a region to participate.) You can join as many regions as you like, but only one region can be marked as your home region.

Why isn’t there a region near me?

We are only able to create regions where we have a local volunteer (called a Municipal Liaison) in place to coordinate for the area. If you’re interested in creating a region in your area and becoming and ML, you can find more information on the join us page.

How do I become a Municipal Liaison for my region?

To become a Municipal Liaison for your region, you must be over 18 years of age and have done (and won!) NaNoWriMo at least once.

For more details on duties and expectations for MLs, please check out our Local Volunteers page. We strongly encourage interested applicants to first contact existing MLs if your region already has an ML(s).

If you want to apply to be a Municipal Liaison, applications will be posted on the Local Volunteer page when they are available. If applications have closed for the year, you can sign up there to be notified when the next round of applications are available.

My Nonprofit

Is my donation tax-deductible?

Yes! NaNoWriMo, Camp NaNoWriMo, and NaNoWriMo's Young Writers Program are projects of National Novel Writing Month, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization based in Berkeley, California. All donations are fully tax-deductible in the United States.

If you are donating from outside the United States, we recommend you check with the relevant authority in your country about whether or not donations to NaNoWriMo are tax-deductible.

How can I volunteer for NaNoWriMo?

We rely on a full-time staff of paid employees for most of the core work of organizing and facilitating the event. If you’d like to volunteer in your area, please consider being a Municipal Liaison. We also hire seasonal interns and have occasional volunteer opportunities.

Please feel free to get in touch and tell us more about what you do, and how it can help NaNoWriMo participants.

Can I use the NaNoWriMo name and/or logo? What’s under your trademark?

We love that you care about NaNoWriMo enough to want to bring more NaNoWriMo stuff into the world! That said, we’ve put a lot of work into making the NaNoWriMo brand a shining beacon of writing encouragement, and we must be protective of our name and logo.

We have legal protection on the names “National Novel Writing Month” and “NaNoWriMo,” as well as our logo and any derivative products. To help us put as much of our resources as possible into our events, we ask that you adhere to the following usage rules:

Logo: Please do not use our logo or parts of our logo.

Manufactured items: Please do not make any products, free or otherwise, bearing the “NaNoWriMo” or “National Novel Writing Month” name.

Flyers: Please don’t make your own. We have official flyers that we’d love you to use to promote NaNoWriMo.

Web and social media graphics: Please don’t make your own. Every year we craft some NaNo Flair that you can add to your website or social media accounts.

Software and applications: Please do not include “NaNoWriMo” or “National Novel Writing Month” in the name of your software or application.
